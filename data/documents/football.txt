Football is one of the most popular sports in the world. It is played between two teams, and each team normally has eleven players. The main objective is to score goals by moving the ball into the opponent's goal. Outfield players normally use their feet, head, and body, while the goalkeeper can use hands inside the penalty area.

A standard football match contains two halves of forty-five minutes. The referee may add extra time for injuries, substitutions, and other delays. The team that scores more goals wins the match. If both teams score the same number of goals, the match may end as a draw. Some knockout competitions use extra time and penalties to decide the winner.

Football players have different roles. The goalkeeper protects the goal. Defenders try to stop attacks and protect dangerous areas. Midfielders connect defense and attack by passing, pressing, and controlling space. Forwards create and finish scoring opportunities. Every player must work with teammates for the team to perform well.

A formation describes the general position of players on the field. Common formations include 4-4-2, 4-3-3, and 3-5-2. Coaches select formations based on the strengths of their players and the weaknesses of the opponent. Formations help teams balance defense, midfield control, width, and attacking power.

Football tactics include possession, counterattacking, pressing, and defensive organization. A possession-based team tries to control the ball through accurate passing. A counterattacking team quickly attacks after winning the ball. Pressing means putting pressure on opponents to force mistakes and recover possession.

Important football skills include passing, shooting, dribbling, tackling, heading, and ball control. Physical fitness is also necessary because players must run, change direction, and perform high-intensity actions throughout the match. Communication, teamwork, discipline, and decision-making are as important as individual talent.

Football also has a strong cultural and social influence. Clubs and national teams can represent local identity, history, and community. Major competitions attract millions of viewers. The sport brings people together, creates employment, and teaches cooperation, resilience, leadership, and respect. The offside rule prevents attacking players from waiting unfairly close to the opponent's goal. A player is not automatically offside only because of position. The decision depends on the player's location and involvement when a teammate plays the ball. The offside rule encourages movement, timing, and organized defending.

Fouls occur when players break the laws of the game through actions such as pushing, tripping, holding, or dangerous tackling. The referee may award a free kick or penalty. Yellow cards are warnings, while red cards remove a player from the match. Referees and assistant referees are responsible for applying the rules fairly.

Set pieces include corner kicks, free kicks, penalties, and throw-ins. Teams often practice set pieces because they provide planned scoring opportunities. Coaches study the opponent's strengths and weaknesses before a match. Modern football also uses video analysis and performance data to examine passing, movement, possession, shots, and defensive actions.
